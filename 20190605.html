1 Elevación solar

De Wikipedia:

The solar zenith angle is the angle between the zenith and the centre of the Sun’s disc. The solar elevation angle is the altitude of the Sun, the angle between the horizon and the centre of the Sun’s disc. Since these two angles are complementary, the cosine of either one of them equals the sine of the other. They can both be calculated with the same formula, using results from spherical trigonometry.

1.1 Fórmulas

\[ \cos \theta_s = \sin \alpha_s = \sin \Phi \sin \delta + \cos \Phi \cos \delta \cos h\](1)

Donde los valores de la ec. 1 son:

  • \(\theta_s\) es el ángulo cenital solar (solar zenith angle)
  • \(\alpha_s\) es el ángulo de elevación solar (solar elevation angle o solar altitude angle) \(\alpha_s=90°–\theta_s\)
  • \(h\) es el ángulo horario en el tiempo solar local.
  • \(\delta\) es la declinación solar actual.
  • \(\Phi\) es la latitud local is the local.
